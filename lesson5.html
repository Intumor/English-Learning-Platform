In this lesson we will learn more about adjectives. We use adjectives to describe things and also compare one thing to another. "(He is stronger than her)", "(She is more beautiful than her mom)". When we deal with shirt adjectives we just add "er" at the end of to turn it into a comparative adjective. In order to say that something is the most, we add "est" at the end of the word and "the" before the word. Usually if something is "the most" it is only one thing, hence the artcile "the"

big - bigger - the biggest
small - smaller - the smallest

Whe we deal with long adjectives we use words "more" to turn it into a comparative adjective and "the most" to turn it into "the most state"

beautiful - more beautiful - the most beautiful

When we compare someone to someone else we use "que" in Spanish, in English it is "than"

I am faster than him
They are more interesting than us

old - viejo
new - nuevo
faster - rapid
slow - decpacito

There is one pattern in all European languages - adjectives "good" and "bad" don't follow this rule. So when it comes to these words we will use a slightly different pattern. Let's take a look at it:

good - better - the best
bad - worse - the worst

So when we use these two adjectives we will need to remember their special form for comparison, all the other ones will follow the rule we talked about above.

Before jumping to the exercises let's cover some of the prepositions, days of the week, months and seasons. First we will focus on talking about temporal prepositions "in" and "ago".
Similar to Spansih when we want to say that something is going to happen after a certain amount of time we use "in" - "we will go to Germany in three years" - "Vendremos a Alemania en tres años"

There is one more thing for us to talk about. Words: "last", "this", "next" don't require prepositions. When you say "Last night", "This evening", "Next Monday" you don't need to use prepositions or article.
