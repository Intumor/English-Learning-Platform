He is loved

Let's take a look at one more sctructure in English. We learned that there three forms of the irregular verb like "see (saw) [seen]"

So the word seen is a past participle, and we can use it in the way we use past participle in Spanish as well

He loves - Él ama
He is loved - Él es amado
He sees - Él ve
He is seen - Él es visto

As we can see the logic is similar, if the verb is regular we add "ed" if irregular we use the third form of that verb. Just like in the structure "i have seen/loved"

Let's take a look at some other examples:

build (built) [built] - construir

The house was built - La casa fue construida
He built a house - Él construyó una casa
The house is being built - La casa está siendo construida
The house will be built - La casa será construida

do (did) [done] - hacer

It was done - Estaba hecho
It will be done - Se hará

clean - limpiar
discuss - discutir

The question will be discussed - La pregunta será discutida
The question was discussed yesterday - La pregunta fue discutida ayer

And last but not least the verb "to be" is sligthly differeny but the past participle of "be" is "been"

i have been to Spain three times - He estado en España tres veces
I have never been to Germany - Nunca he estado en Alemania

Don't worry if you can't remember all the forms the irregular verbs right away, with time you will get them all. After all there are not so many of them. Ninety percent of all the verbs arer irregular only a small portion of them is irregular, they are however the most popular ones, which makes it easier to remember them since you will be encountering them all the time

What's next?

As you can see we can add more words to the sentence making it more complex, however the structure stays he same, that's why it's important to know it

In order ro retain all that we've learned here it is importantto associate everything you hear with somethingand try to visualize what you hear. When someone says "i will visit my grandma next year" you should the able to visualize someone visiting their grandma one year from now. That is the best way to rememberthe words, expression and so on. And remember word means something only in some context, don't try to memorize lists of words that is a very ineffective way to learn. They way you want to approach it is by consuming content you like. Any time you come across a new word, you translate it to your native language or you can even try to guess what it means first, that would teach your brain how to learn words from the context (you can ask ChatGPT for 5 sentences using that word and try to guess what it means from those examples), you will learn a lot of words this way as well, without having to look them up. And when you get the word you write it down, and try to use it in a sentence, or two. You don't have to write this sentence down, but it would be great if you could say it out loud. That way you will be involving your visual memory, motoric memory and your listening based memory. It will increase chances of you retaining this word. You don't have to go over the words or anything (although it is up to you, review words that you learned is also a great tool that i have used myself quite successfully), you just keep going forward, keep reading or watching and keep doing this, the point is that you will keep coming across all of those words and over time you will have memorized all of them without cramming or anything like that. You will forget some of them, it's impossible to memorize everything the first time you see it unless you have an extroadinary memory, but just rememeber that it's a natural part of learning. Sometimes you will have to come across the same word twenty times before it sticks with you, it's normal.
