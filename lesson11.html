In this lesson we will cover some more ground in basics. We will learn a new very useful structure in English. So we have gone over basic verb structure, we have taken a look at the structure of the verb "to be". We have learned about he form that emphasizes thatb the action is happening at a certain moment. Now let's take a look at the structure that we will use when we're talking about some kind of result of an action.

For regular verbs the structure is going to be like this:

[pronoun] + [to have] + [verb in the past, with "ed" ending]

I have loved
She has talked
You have managed

For irregular verbs the structure is going to be like this:

[pronoun] + [to have] + [verb's third form]

I have seen
She has spoken
You have gone

You already know about irregular verbs and that we use the second form for making a positive statement in the past. All irregular verbs have three forms though, and the third form is used in this case where we talk about some kind of results

We have a similar structure in Spanish

I see - Yo veo
I saw - Yo vi
I have seen - Yo he visto

So when we talk about something that happened in the past and matters right now we use this structure. Let's take a look at some examples


see (saw) [seen] - ver

"Do you want to watch "Spider-Man?"
"No, i have seen this movie already"


So here when the person says "i have seen it", it's not just a simple fact, that it happened, they're saying that thye already have the information about this movie, they know it, they know what they're talking about, they've already had this experience

"Have you ever loved?"

Here a person is asking about an experience as well. one thing you might have noticed here is that "have" and the pronoun switched places to build a question. It's just a special feature of this tense. When it comes to asking qustion in this tense we just put "to have" before the pronoun

"Has he seen you?"
"Have you talked to her?"

make (made) [made] - hacer

"Do you want to get a beer with me?"
"No, i have already made some plans"

So here a person is talking about results of his actions. He made plans in the past but they matter right now, so the past affects the person's response, the present

When it comes to using the verb "to be" in that structure we will use the third form as well which is "been"

I have been to Germany many times
She has been a doctor for 3 years

Probably you've noticed that the verb "make" has the same exact second and third form. Some verbs are like that. For example the verb "cut" (cortar) is the same in all three forms - cut (cut) [cut]. You will eventually commit all of them to memory. There are not that many irregular verbs, however they are the most popular ones.

From now on, if the verb is irregular, you will see all three forms of it.

Feelings

In Spanish we have one way to express feelings, in English there's a different one. In Spanish we say that we have a certain feeling or emotion, but in English you describe yourself using an adjective related to that emotion or feeling.

I am afraid - Tengo miedo
I am hungry - Tengo hambre
I am cold - Tengo frío

So when we say that in English we literally say: estoy hambriento, estoy frio

So that is the proper structure for describing emotions and feelings in English. Of course i should mention that in Spanish we can use a similar structure as in English, but in English it will be the same:

I am tired - Estoy cansado
I am angry - Estoy enojado

Weather

When we talk about weather in English we use words that describe temperature or other phenomena and use this structure: It is ...

it will be cold - va a hacer frío
it's hot - hace calor
it was dark - estaba oscuro

if we are talking about weather conditions the structure remains the same:

it's raining - está lloviendo
it's snowing - está nevando
it's windy - hace viento
