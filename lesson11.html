In this lesson we will cover some more ground in basics. We will learn a new very useful structure in English. So we have gone over basic verb structure, we have taken a look at the structure of the verb "to be". We have learned about he form that emphasizes thatb the action is happening at a certain moment. Now let's take a look at the structure that we will use when we're talking about some kind of result of an action.

For regular verbs the structure is going to be like this:

[pronoun] + [to have] + [verb in the past, with "ed" ending]

I have loved
She has talked
You have managed

For irregular verbs the structure is going to be like this:

[pronoun] + [to have] + [verb's third form]

I have seen
She has spoken
You have gone

You already know about irregular verbs and that we use the second form for making a positive statement in the past. All irregular verbs have three forms though, and the third form is used in this case where we talk about some kind of results

We have a similar structure in Spanish

I see - Yo veo
I saw - Yo vi
I have seen - Yo he visto

So when we talk about something that happened in the past and matters right now we use this structure. Let's take a look at some examples


see (saw) [seen] - ver

"Do you want to watch "Spider-Man?"
"No, i have seen this movie already"


So here when the person says "i have seen it", it's not just a simple fact, that it happened, they're saying that thye already have the information about this movie, they know it, they know what they're talking about, they've already had this experience

"Have you ever loved?"

Here a person is asking about an experience as well

make (made) [made] - hacer

"Do you want to get a beer with me?"
"No, i have already made some plans"

So here a person is talking about results of his actions. He made plans in the past but they matter right now, so the past affects the person's response, the present
