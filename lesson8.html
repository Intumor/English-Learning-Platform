Before we get into practice i want to talk about one more thing. The difference between "in" and "at". When we say "i am in school" or anywhere else, "in the house", "in the car" and so on, we mean that we are inside of this thing. When we say "i am at school", "at work", "at home" we don't mean exactly being inside of those things, we mean being in the visinity of them. If i have a house and i have a garden on my property, even when i am in the garden i am still at home. I am not on the house but i am however at home. When i am at school, i could somewhere outside of the school building. I could be out at the quad, or school stadium, but if i get phone call asking me where i am i will still say "at school" and if i say "in the school" i would imply that i am in the school building. When i say "i am at the car" i mean that i am around the around, not literally inside of it, but somewhere near it.

Phrasal verbs

In this section we will be taking a look at phrasal verbs. In English phrasal verbs are used all the time, whereas in Spanish we usually have a separate word for what you're trying to say, but in English you use the same verbs but the we add prepositions to them to change or augment their meaning. I think it's easier to see how it work with some examples


Let's take a verb "go"


I go - Yo voy
I go up - Subo
I go down - Bajo
I go back - Vuelvo
Go away - Vete
I go in - Entro
I go out - Salgo



I look - Yo miro
I look up - Miro hacia arriba
I look down - Miro hacia abajo
I look back - Miro hacia atrás
Look away - Mira hacia otro lado
I look in - Miro adentro
I look out - Miro afuera
I look for - Busco



I come - Yo vengo
I come up - Subo
I come down - Bajo
I come back - Vuelvo
I come in - Entro
I come out - Salgo



I take - Yo tomo
I take back - Me retracto
I take out - Yo saco
Take out the trash - Saca la basura



This, in a nutshell, is how the phrasal verbs work. Not all of the phrasal verbs are like that, sometimes the meaning is not obvious. For example: "Come up" generally means "ocurrirse", and a verb "Put up" means "toleraré"



I came up with an idea - Se me ocurrió una idea
I don't have to put up with this - No tengo que aguantar esto
