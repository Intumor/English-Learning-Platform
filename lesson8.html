Look, See, and Watch

In this section we'll talk about the difference between verbs "look", "see" and "watch"

See - Ver

"See" is more about the natural act of perceiving things with your eyes without necessarily making a conscious effort to do so.


"I can see the mountains from my window" - "Puedo ver las montañas desde mi ventana"


"See" can imply that something is within your field of vision or that you have the ability to perceive it visually.

Look - Mirar

"Look" is used when you intentionally direct your eyes towards something to observe it or to search for something specific.


"Please look at that painting on the wall" - "Por favor, mira ese cuadro en la pared"


When you use "look," you are actively engaging your sense of sight to focus on something. Usually when we use look we use a preposition "at", as in the example above

Watch - Observar

"Watch" involves actively paying attention to something over a period of time, often involving movement or changes.


"Let's watch the sunset" - "Vamos a observar el atardecer"


When you "watch" something, you are usually focusing your attention on it for a duration, such as watching a movie, a sports game, or a performance.

"Listen" vs "Hear"

Hear - Oír

When we use "hear" we usually mean the ability to hear, just like we mean the physycal ability to see when we use "see"


"I can't hear you, the volume is too low" - "No puedo oírte, el volumen está demasiado bajo"

Listen - Escuchar

When use "listen" we mean paying attention yo what you hear. Maybe someone is hearing you, but not listening to you. So if they "hear" you they can just physycaly hear that you're talking, but if they are "listening" to you, it means that they are paying attention to what you are saying


"You should always listen to your parents" - "Siempre debes escuchar a tus padres"

Second thing i wanted to talk about is the fluency mechanism itself. Some people even English teachers will tell you that vocabulary is the main factor when it comes to fluency, but it couldn't be further away from the truth. Take a five-year-old in your native language. They don't have a huge vocabulary, your vocabulary probably is lot richer, you can express yourself a lot more eloquently, accurately, but i'm not saying freely. becasue a fuve-year-old is pretty confident and liberal with the language. They don't struggle with words, they don't say "i don't how to say this in Spanish/English", they jsut find some words to say what they want to say. Sometimes it doesn't sound as good or accurate as the speech of an adult but they get the job done. This is the point. Even if you don't have a huge vocabulary, don't be afraid to speak, this is how we learn.
