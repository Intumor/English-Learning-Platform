To - In order to

When we need to say that someone did something to do somerthing else like "He came to talk" we use the word "to" in English which also means "para" in Spanish

i came to talk to you - Vine para hablar contigo
He came to speak - Él vino para hablar

Let - dejar

Several lessons ago we learned abou the phrase "let's" in English. There is another meaning to that word. "Let" also means "dejar". So when we say "let's go" it is short for "let us go" which literally means "allow us to go". Let's take a look at some examples of how you could use it:

Let me go - Déjame ir
Let him go - Déjalo ir
Let her do it - Déjala hacerlo

Modal verbs

There are a handful of phrasal verbs in English, but in this lesson we will only focus on two of them for now

can - poder
should - deber

Modal verbs are not like any other ones, they don't follow the same rules

First - Modal verbs do not change. "Can" will always be "can" regardless of the pronoun before it

I can do it - Puedo hacerlo
We can do it - Podemos hacerlo
He can do it - Él puede hacerlo

Second - in order to ask a question like "¿Puede hacerlo ella?" we will not be using "do" as we did with the other verbs. In order to make a question using a modal verb we will swap the pornoun with a modal verb

Can you do it? - ¿Puedes hacerlo?
Can we do it? - ¿Podemos hacerlo?
Can he do it? - ¿Puede hacerlo él?

Third - in order to use it in a negative form we will add "not" after the verb. So "No puedo" would be "I cannot" or "i can't" for short. The short form is much more commonly used in English.

I can't do it - No puedo hacerlo
They can't do it - Ellos no pueden hacerlo
She can't do it - Ella no puede hacerlo

Also, there is one more thing worth mentioning: In Spanish we can say "Se puede hacer", however we can't yse thye same structure in English, we always have to use a pronoun to indicate who can do it. "I can do it", "He can do it" and so on. But if we wanted to make a phrase using a more neutral pronoun we can say "One can do it" which means it could be done by anybody. If you saw "Lord of the Rings" you probably remember that scene where Boromoir says "One does not simply walk into Mordor". It's not a commonly used structure though

Can i smoke? - ¿Puedo fumar?
Can i go out? - ¿Puedo salir?
Can i speak with you? - ¿Puedo hablar contigo?

You can do it! - ¡Tú puedes hacerlo!
You can drink a glass of water - Puedes tomar un vaso de agua

Should i go? - ¿Debería ir?
Should i do it? - ¿Debería hacerlo?
Should i go or should i stay? - ¿Debería ir o debería quedarme?

I think you should do it - Creo que deberías hacerlo
I think you should go there - Creo que deberías ir allí

Notice how we don't use the word "that" in English. Even though we could say "I think that you should do it", it's not necessary. Native speakers tend not to use that word that often

One last thing before you go. There is a phrase in English that is used fairly often which is really good to know. the phrase is "or else", which translates as "o si no" or "de lo contrario"

"Don't it, or else" - "No hazlo, o si no"
