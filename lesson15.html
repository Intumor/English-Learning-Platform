Here are some question to help you put together a story:

  1. What is your favorite food?
  2. How often do you eat it?
  3. What food do you dislike?
  4. What food is popular in your country?
  5. Have you tried growing your own food? How did it go?
  6. If you could only eat one food for the rest of your life, what would you choose?
  7. Do you know any vegetarians? What food do they not eat?
  8. Do you like cooking?
  9. What is your favorite food to make?
  10. What kind of food is unhealthy?
  11. What food respresents you?
  12. How often do you eat at restaurants?

You can say a lot by now! Of course there's always room for improvement but you should give yourself some credit for achieving all of this! Going forward i have one more recommendation: learning new words. The best is to learn new words from the context. We will talk about it in more detail in the next lesson, for now i just wanted to tell about it. Learning words from the context develops the intuitive side of the language understanding and it recreates the way we aquired our native language when we were little. You don't always have to look up words and expressions you see or hear for the first time, some of them will just stick with you if you hear them over and over again.
