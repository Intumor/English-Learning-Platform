Before we get to the juicy part, there are a few more things i want to mention.

First: when we talk about work we often use the phrase "I work for [company]" which translates to "(spanish)". So when somebody asks what you do for a living you can say "I work for Google" or "I work for my uncle"

Second: when we want to name a profession, we can often do that by adding "er" to a verb.

For example: write - writer, drive - driver.

It doesn't always mean a profession, sometimes it's just someone who does this thing.

View - viewer, listen - listener, play - player

Articles

First things first, we have to understand what articles mean and why we even bother to use them

The article "a" is short for "one". So when you say "a dog" it is short for "one dog". So it means one out of many. Sometimes you might see "an". It's usually used when the article is followed by a vowel sound, just to make it sound better

"the" is short for "this", so when we say "the dog" it is short for "this dog". So this means this particular dog, not a random one, specific dog

a/an - one
the - this
