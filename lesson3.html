Verb "To be"

Verb "to be" is another equally important structure that we have to get familiar with. Remember howi told that by mastering the "Basic Verb Scructure you will master half of the language"?  Past

To sum it up:

- With "I" we use "am" in the Present tense - i am smart - yo soy ...

- With "You", "We", "They" we use "are" in the Present tense - We are beautiful - ...

- With "He", "She", "It" we use "is" - He is ugly - ...

- In order to ask a question in the Present tense we flip "am/are/is" and a pronoun - are we tired?

- In order to create a negative sentence in the Present tense, we add "not" to "am/are/is" - i am not be tired

- In the Future tense use "will be" with all the pronouns - i will be tired

- In order to ask a question in the Future tense we flip "will" and a pronoun - Will i be tired?

- In order to create a negative sentence in the Future tense, we add "not" to "will" - i will not be tired

- In the Past tense we use "was" with "I", "He", "She", "It" - she was stupid

- And we use "were" for "You", "We", "They" - we were lazy

- In order to ask a question in the Past tense we flip "was/were" and a pronoun - were they be tired?

- In order to create a negative sentence in the Past tense, we add "not" to "was/were" - he was not tired

Infitive

El infinitivo es la forma base de un verbo. Es la forma del verbo que encontrarías en un diccionario, y no está cambiada para coincidir con un sujeto específico o un tiempo verbal. Por ejemplo, en inglés, 'to walk' es el infinitivo del verbo 'caminar'. Es como la raíz del verbo antes de que se le agreguen las terminaciones para indicar quién está haciendo la acción o cuándo está ocurriendo. En español, los infinitivos terminan en '-ar', '-er', o '-ir', como 'hablar', 'comer' y 'vivir'.

For example:

To speak - hablar

I want to speak
I like to dance

Another thing worth mentioning is how we use words like "like" in English
In English any action, any verb will always go in the direction away from the verb. In Spanish it goes both ways. Yo veo ⇢ | I see ⇢ | Me gusta ⇠ el | I like ⇢ him
