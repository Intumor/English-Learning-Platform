Verb "To be"

Verb "to be" is another equally important structure that we have to get familiar with. Remember howi told that by mastering the "Basic Verb Scructure you will master half of the language"? As you probably noticed from the exercises - we can't say anything without using the basic verb structure. This To be Structure is gonna be similar in that way. The main difference being is that we use Basic Verb Scructure to talk about actions and To be Structure is used to describe things. Let's take a closer look at it.

We have the same table with the same rules. Three possible ways of making a sentence: positive statement, negative statement and inquiry, and three tenses: Future, Present and Past

To sum it up:

- For "I" we use "am"

- For "You", "We", "They" we use "are"

- For "He", "She", "It" we use "is"

- In the Future tense use "will be" with all the pronouns

- In the Past tense we use "was" for "I", "He", "She", "It"

- And we use "were" for "You", "We", "They"
