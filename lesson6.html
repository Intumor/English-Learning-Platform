"How many" vs "How much"

We use how "much" with something we can't count:

time, money, love, water

Before you say "Oh, wait but i can count money!" i will say you can't. You can count dollars, pesos, euros, rupees, but we can't coun't money, it's something abstract

We use how "many" with something we can count:

days, hours, dollars, people

Other ways to say "Cuanto"

As you have probably noticed from the last exercise, in English we don't have the word "Cuanto", instead we use the word "how" coupled with another word related to what we are asking about. Here are some of them:

  • How long
  • How often
  • How far
  • How fast
  • How old
  • How much
  • How many

So if we want to ask someone how old they are we will say "How old are you?" which literally translates to "(spanish)". Or if we wanna ask how much time something will take we will say "How long will it take?", or if we want to ask how often something happens we wil also use this construction "How often do you come here?"

Other tricky adjectives

We have some other tricky words in English that could be confusing for Spanish speakers: "little" and "few", and "little" and "small", which could all be translated to Spanish as "poco", however we can't use them interchangably

When it comes to "little" vs "few", we use little with uncountables:

time, compassion, patience

And we use how "few" with countables:

people, cats, cars, animals

When it comes to "little" vs "small". We can also use little when we talk about someone's age, meaning they are young:

i saw two little girls playing outside

And we use how "small" when we refer to size:

These shoes are small
