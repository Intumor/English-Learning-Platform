Now let's practice with dates. In order to say a date in English we need to follow a certain format. In American English tthe format is like this: Month/Day/Year. So if you want to say "17 de julio" you say iy like this "July Seventeenth" or "July the Seventeenth". Both forms are acceptable, although tne first is more common in informal situations. There is also a Europian date format: Day/Month/Year. In that case "17 de julio" would be "Seventeenth of July" in this format. You can also use "The Seventeenth of July" but again it's less common in spoken English.
